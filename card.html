Eagle

Like all birds of prey, eagles have very large, hooked beaks for ripping flesh from their prey, strong, muscular legs, and powerful talons. The beak is typically heavier than that of most other birds of prey.

  • Fish eagles: Haliaeetus, Ichthyophaga
  • Booted eagles: Aquila, Hieraaetus; Spizaetus, Oroaetus, Spizastur; Nisaetus
  • Snake eagles: Circaetus, Spilornis, Dryotriorchis, Terathopius
  • Harpy eagles: Harpy eagles,Philippine eagle,Solitary eagles

Eagle is a common name for many large birds of prey of the family Accipitridae; it belongs to several groups of genera that are not necessarily closely related to each other. Most of the 60 species of eagles are from Eurasia and Africa.
